Cinecase — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026

Cinecase ("the app", "we") is a local‑first movie/Blu‑ray collection app. This policy explains what data the app handles, where it goes, and the choices you have. In plain terms: your collection lives on your device, we run no server, and we do not sell or monetise your data.

Who we are

Developer: Canadilian Soft
Contact: canadiliansoft@gmail.com

The short version

  • Your movie collection is stored on your device. No account is required to

    • use the app.

  • We operate no servers and have no database of your data. We cannot see

    • your collection.

  • The app contains **no advertising, no analytics SDKs, and no third‑party

    • trackers**.

  • Some features send data to third‑party services (TMDb, a barcode lookup

    • service, and — only if you turn on cloud sync — your own Google Drive). These are described below.

What stays on your device

  • Your libraries, movies, ratings, watch status, loans, tags, and settings are

    • stored locally (in an on‑device database). Uninstalling the app deletes them.

  • The app also writes periodic local backup files to its own private app

    • storage so you can restore after an accident. These never leave your device unless you share or sync them.

Third‑party services the app uses

1. The Movie Database (TMDb) — When you search for a title or scan a disc, the app sends the search text (and, for scans, the looked‑up title/year) to TMDb to fetch movie details and poster images. Poster images load from TMDb's public image CDN. See TMDb's privacy policy at themoviedb.org. This product uses the TMDb API but is not endorsed or certified by TMDb.

2. Barcode lookup (UPCitemdb / optional Barcode Lookup) — When you scan a disc, the app sends the barcode number to a barcode database to identify the product. No personal information is attached to these requests.

3. Camera — The camera is used only to scan barcodes. Frames are processed on your device to read the barcode; images are not stored or transmitted.

4. Google Sign‑In and Google Drive (optional cloud sync) — If you turn on cloud sync, you sign in with Google and the app stores a backup of your collection in your own Google Drive (a private app‑data area of your account). We have no server and cannot access your Drive. Your Google email address is used to show which account is connected and is cached on your device. You can sign out at any time and delete the backup from your Drive.

5. Google Play Billing (Android) / the App Store (iOS) — The optional one‑time "Cinecase Pro" purchase is processed by Google or Apple. The app only records whether Pro is unlocked; we never receive your payment details.

What we collect

We, the developer, collect nothing. There is no developer server, account system, analytics, or tracking. Data is only ever transmitted to the third‑party services listed above to make a feature work, or to your own Google Drive if you enable sync. All such transmission uses encrypted (HTTPS) connections.

Sharing your collection

Export and "share my shelf" features send a file (JSON/CSV/PDF/image) only when you choose to, through your device's standard share sheet, to the destination you pick.

Your choices and data deletion

  • Delete items / libraries in the app at any time.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all on‑device data and local backups.
  • Cloud sync: sign out of Google in Settings, and delete the Cinecase backup

    • from your Google Drive, to remove the synced copy.

  • Export your full collection at any time so your data is portable.

Children

Cinecase is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country) and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, noted in the app or store listing.
